## Overview

The Daily Journal helps you reflect on each day, track your energy, practice gratitude, and capture learnings. Journaling is integrated into your daily [Startup and Wind-Down Routines](/docs/features/routines) for consistent reflection habits.

### Key Capabilities

- **Gratitude practice**: Daily appreciation reflection
- **Energy tracking**: Monitor energy levels over time
- **Learning capture**: Document daily insights
- **Challenge reflection**: Process difficulties constructively
- **Rich text editing**: Full formatting support
- **Day-based organization**: Entries tied to specific dates

## Journal Sections

### Gratitude

The gratitude section helps you cultivate appreciation:

**Prompt**: "What are you grateful for today?"

Benefits:
- Shifts focus to positive aspects
- Improves overall well-being
- Creates historical gratitude record

### Energy Reflection

Track your daily energy levels:

**Prompt**: "How was your energy level today?"

Use this to:
- Identify energy patterns
- Correlate energy with activities
- Optimize your schedule

### Learning & Growth

Capture daily insights:

**Prompt**: "What did I learn today?"

Document:
- New knowledge acquired
- Skills practiced
- Insights gained

### Challenges

Process difficulties constructively with a three-part framework:

1. **Mistakes**: "Where did you fall from grace?"
2. **Causes**: "What caused that?"
3. **Solutions**: "The next time this happens, what can you do about it?"

This transforms challenges into growth opportunities.

## Using the Journal

### In Startup Routine

Morning journaling sets intentions:

1. Navigate to **Startup Routine**
2. Complete reflection sections
3. Set daily outcomes
4. Review your mindset

### In Wind-Down Routine

Evening journaling captures the day:

1. Navigate to **Wind-Down**
2. Complete gratitude section
3. Reflect on energy levels
4. Document learnings
5. Process any challenges

### Standalone Journal

For freeform writing:

1. Navigate to **Journal**
2. Use the rich text editor
3. Write freely without prompts
4. Content saves automatically

## Best Practices

### Consistency

- Journal at the same time daily
- Start with gratitude (positive framing)
- Keep entries focused but complete
- Don't skip days (brief entry better than none)

### Gratitude Practice

- Write 3 specific gratitudes daily
- Vary between big and small appreciations
- Include people, experiences, and things
- Be specific rather than generic

### Energy Tracking

- Rate energy on a 1-10 scale
- Note factors affecting energy
- Track patterns over time
- Adjust schedule based on insights

### Learning Capture

- Document one key learning daily
- Include how you'll apply it
- Connect to goals when relevant
- Review learnings weekly

### Challenge Processing

- Write without judgment
- Focus on controllable factors
- Create actionable solutions
- Follow up on improvements

## Privacy
